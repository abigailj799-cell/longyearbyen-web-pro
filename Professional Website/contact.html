FAQ

Q. Why is it illegal to die in Longyearbyen?
A. It isn't illegal to die, just highly discouraged, and rather it is illegal be buried in the town or around, as it can preserve old diseases and illnesses, and it is an absolute pain to dig into the frozen ground.
Q. What is the best time to visit?
A. It's nice to visit all year round, with each season offering differing and unique experiences! It honestly depends on what you want to see or experience when visiting.
Q. Do I need a passport, visa, or other travel documents to visit?
A. All visitors must present a passport or national ID card. This applies to visitors from other countries and also Norwegian citizens.
Q. What currency is used in Svalbard and Longyearbyen?
A. The currency used is the Norwegian kroner (NOK), and most shops prefer the use of cards over cash. Cash cannot be exchanged in Longyearbyen, but businesses accept all virtual credit cards as mean of payment. There is no bank, cash dispenser, or ATM in Svalbard, so if you are going to withdraw cash, do it on the mainland of Norway before travel.
Q. Where can you encounter Polar Bears in Longyearbyen?
A. You can encounter them year-round, and you must be extremely cautious when moving outside of the settlement. When outside, you should travel with an experienced local guide who has the proper equipment for polar bear protection (scaring devices and firearms) and a proficiency in using them.
Q. Can you take reindeer antlers home?
A. You can, only if they are shed. Antlers still attached to the skull are considered to be a part of the wildlife, and are prohibited from being taken.

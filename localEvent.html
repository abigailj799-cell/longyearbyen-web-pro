Longyearbyen has quite a lot of local events, such as the dark season blues festival, which starts in late october, lasting for 4 days, marking the start of the dark season, where svalbard has no sun for 4 months, and the blue light of the 'twilight period' adds a very unique atmosphere, and many different concerts are held by local, national, and international blues artists. Some more information can be read at: https://www.svalbardblues.com

During the 1st week of March, they celebrate the Solfestuka (sun festival week), to celebrate the 'return of the sun' from the dark months. It usually starts on march 8th, with people gathering on old hospital steps in order to celebrate, with a saying that is 'the sun is declared back in longyearbyen when its rays reach the steps'. It usually has lots of concerts and other various forms of entertainment, and many smaller events during it.
